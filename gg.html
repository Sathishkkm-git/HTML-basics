Introduction of Organic Farming:-

What is Organic Farming?

Organic farming is an agricultural method that includes the production of crops and livestock by using natural means instead of spraying pesticides, use of fertilizers, antibiotics, genetic modifications of growth hormones and organisms.

Home & Garden

  • Indoor Gardening

    • Indoor gardening is growing a selection of plants inside that you would typically grow outside, such as fruit, vegetables, herbs, or flowers1. It can be done in containers, pots, or other types of vessels, such as terrariums

  • Terrace Garden

    • Terrace gardening is set of activities to grow one’s own food on the terrace of the house. We can grow everything from cereals, pulses, herbs, vegetables and fruits.

  • Urban Farms

    • Urban Farming is also known as urban agriculture. It means growing crops and raising small livestock or milk cows in small areas like vacant plots, gardens, verges, balconies and containers.

  • Natural Resources

    • Natural resource management is a discipline in the management of natural resources such as land, water, soil, plants, and animals—with a particular focus on how management affects quality of life for present and future generations.

What is Organic Fertilizer? Why Should I Use It?

Organic fertilizer is derived from organic matter. It comprises once-living things and includes vegetables, animal matter, and manures. Organic fertilizers are much better for your health and the environment, so you should use them over chemically produced fertilizers
